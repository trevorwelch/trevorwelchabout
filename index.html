autobiography

I'm Trevor Welch, I was born in 1988 in Castro Valley, CA and I'm currently living in Chicago. I'm a composer, music producer, and budding machine learning enthusiast. I grew up studying classical piano, then jazz, later playing in rock bands. In 2007, I interned with Grammy-award winning producer/engineer Jim Scott (Wilco, The Rolling Stones, Tom Petty and the Heartbreakers, etc.). I left the studio in 2008 to attend California State University Northridge's film scoring program. That year I worked with David Newman on a film music in concert event, and also wrote a lot of music for picture in my classes / with student directors. I left CSUN in 2009 to study composition and piano performance at Westmont College, and graduated with a B.A. in Music in 2011. I composed/recorded/conducted a number of independent films. Later that year, my friend Alex and I started releasing electronic music under the name Tommy Noble, touring a little bit and playing some festivals with artists like Tiesto, Porter Robinson, Steve Aoki, and others. Since then, I wrote/performed a multimedia and experimental music project at the Mengi Gallery in Reykjavik, Iceland under the name 'Kanaya Base'. Summer 2016, I completed executive producer duties for an album in Chicago with artist Brian Fresco, from Savemoney (Chance the Rapper, Vic Mensa, Towkio, etc.) and attended a weeklong course at Stanford University at the CCRMA Department of Music. This Winter 2017 I completed work writing for the play 'Hot Belly' in Austin, TX and for a short film "YES" by Lorenzo Gattorna in Chicago. I recently ended my residency at Refuge Arts in Wisconsin, where i had a large chapel with a vibraphone, Steinway, CP-70, and field organ all to myself! I wrote music in collaboration with Lorenzo Gattorna for his new film, "HATTA".
